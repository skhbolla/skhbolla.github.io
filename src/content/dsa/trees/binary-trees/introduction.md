A binary tree is a hierarchical data structure in which each node has at most two children, referred to as the left child and the right child.

## Core Properties

* **Max Children:** Every node has `0`, `1`, or `2` children.
* **Recursive Definition:** A tree is either empty, or consists of a root node and two disjoint binary trees (left subtree and right subtree).

In the next part, we will look at how to traverse these structures.
